Kidney Stone Symptoms and When to See a Doctor

Kidney stones are a common condition affecting many adults. Symptoms include severe pain in the side or lower back, burning during urination, nausea and blood in urine.

If the stone blocks urine flow or causes severe pain, consult a urologist immediately. Modern procedures such as RIRS (Retrograde Intrarenal Surgery) and PCNL allow safe and effective removal of kidney stones.

Diet Tips to Prevent Kidney Stones

Drinking plenty of water is the most important way to prevent kidney stones. Patients should aim for at least 2–3 litres of water daily.

Reducing salt intake, limiting oxalate-rich foods such as spinach and maintaining a balanced diet can help reduce recurrence of kidney stones.

Symptoms of Enlarged Prostate (BPH)

Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia is common in men above 50 years. Symptoms include weak urine flow, frequent urination at night, urgency and incomplete emptying of the bladder.

Early consultation with a urologist can prevent complications such as urinary retention or kidney damage. Modern laser prostate surgery provides effective relief.

Urinary Tract Infection in Men

Urinary tract infections cause burning during urination, frequent urge to pass urine and discomfort in the lower abdomen.

Proper diagnosis and treatment by a urologist is important to prevent recurrent infections and complications.
